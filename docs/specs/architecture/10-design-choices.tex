\section{Rationale, one by one}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em]
  \item \textbf{Cortex pattern over a single ``research agent''} --- each cortex owns its tools, skills, SQL helpers. Isolated blast radius, parallel development, trivially testable.
  \item \textbf{Swarm of nanos over a single strong model} --- parallel cheap retrieval, stronger curator on the back end. Orders of magnitude cheaper, bounded latency, easier to cap.
  \item \textbf{Skills as files, not strings} --- version control for prompts, diffable in PRs, reviewable by analysts and compliance.
  \item \textbf{Redis-backed findings with explicit review step} --- Sweep doesn't write blindly. The human is part of the control loop, not an exception path.
  \item \textbf{Repo = queries, service = logic} --- strict separation. Repos do targeted joins; services aggregate and enforce rules. Layers are independently testable.
  \item \textbf{Monorepo over two services} --- shared types and schema are load-bearing. Versioning two npm packages would have bought pain, not isolation.
  \item \textbf{Guardrails in code, not in prompts} --- cost caps, depth limits, hallucination checks live in the orchestrator. The LLM cannot silently exceed its budget or leak sensitive data.
  \item \textbf{Multi-provider by construction} --- per-step model selection. Strong models on planning/synthesis, long-context open models (Kimi, Mistral, LLaMA) on reasoning, fleets of cheap nanos on retrieval. Sensitive workloads can be pinned to self-hosted weights without touching the orchestrator.
\end{enumerate}
